###  Forwarder & Handler Integrations

###  Trucking & Domestic Shipment Workflows

Manage standard road transport shipments from sender to receiver:

- **Sender & Receiver Management**: Capture shipper, consignee, pickup, and delivery parties
- **Goods & Services**: Register goods lines, handling requirements, and operational services per shipment
- **Rates & Pricing**: Apply buy/sell rates, transport tariffs, and service-based charges
- **Execution Tracking**: Follow collection, transit, delivery, and handover milestones

###  Passenger Transport & Emergency (PERS) Handling

Handle passenger and emergency-response operations within the same shipment and dispatch platform:

- **Passenger Transportation**: Support dedicated shipment types and workflows for passenger and person transport
- **PERS / Emergency Handling**: Manage personal emergency response system operations with emergency-specific shipment functionality
- **Operational Data**: Capture instructions, remarks, key information, device IDs, apartment access, trigger types, and deployed resources directly on the shipment
- **Dispatch & Planning**: Combine passenger/PERS orders with vehicle assignment, timing, manifests, and distance/duration handling
- **Johanniter Use Cases**: Support Johanniter-related operational and status-handling scenarios in passenger and PERS contexts

### ️ Auto-Created Manifests & Tours

Plan and build executable transport tours automatically instead of dispatching every truck manually:

- **Automatic Tour Creation**: Generate transport manifests and tours from selected shipments and planning rules
- **Slot-Time Planning**: Respect pickup and delivery slot times or broader time-window constraints during route creation
- **Truck Capacity Limits**: Balance shipments against truck payload, maximum volume, and available Euro-pallet capacity
- **Manifest Output**: Convert optimized routes into transport manifests with ordered stops, timings, and shipment references
- **Fleet & Subcontractor Planning**: Build tours against in-house vehicles or subcontractor resources depending on the planning setup

###  AWB, BOL & AI Document Import

Handle air and ocean transport documents as part of the shipment workflow instead of outside the operational system:

- **Air Waybill (AWB) Management**: Maintain AWB-specific shipment data including shipper, consignee, notify party, ground handler, carrier, agent, flight segments, goods, services, and charges
- **Bill of Lading (BOL) Management**: Manage BOL-specific parties, carrier data, containers, goods, references, services, and commercial shipment details
- **House vs. Master Handling**: Distinguish between house and master documents, including **HAWB** versus **MAWB** on the AWB side and **Bill of Lading** versus **Master Bill of Lading** on the ocean side
- **Back-to-Back Workflows**: Support one-to-one execution where a single house shipment or document is linked directly to a single master AWB or master BOL
- **Consol Workflows**: Support consolidated execution where multiple house AWBs or BOL-based shipment records are grouped under one master document
- **Shipment-Type Integration**: Activate AWB or BOL extensions through the shipment type so the right document structure and fields are available in the shipment
- **AI Document Import**: Import shipment, AWB, and BOL data from free text, emails, and PDF documents using AI-assisted extraction
- **Automatic Population**: Apply imported AWB/BOL data directly into shipment parties, goods, containers, services, references, and charges for faster operational handling

###  Warehouse Shipment Fulfillment

Control inbound and outbound warehouse flows from planning through execution:

- **Planned Goods**: Define incoming and outgoing goods before physical handling starts
- **Allocated Goods**: Reserve stock or expected stock against shipment demand
- **Picking Lists**: Generate warehouse picking tasks for outbound execution
- **Goods Receipt & Goods Issue**: Confirm inbound intake and outbound release with full traceability
- **Package & Product Expiry Dates**: Store separate packaging/package and product expiry dates on warehouse goods for receipt, stock, allocation, picking, and issue flows
- **Inventory Linkage**: Keep warehouse transactions aligned with shipment progress and stock status

###  Shipment Type, Layout & Functionality

Configure shipment behavior by shipment type instead of hard-coding one process for every operation:

- **Layout Relation**: Shipment types define how the shipment screen is laid out, including single-table and expanded workflow variants
- **Field & Table Visibility**: Activate sender, receiver, goods, planned goods, services, prices, lot numbers, and expiry-date fields per shipment type
- **Operational Functionality**: Control which shipment actions are available, including scanning, warehouse execution, auto-created tours/manifests, AWB/BOL extensions, AI imports, delivery documents, labels, picklists, and subcontractor workflows
- **Process-Specific Behavior**: Use different shipment types to tailor air cargo, trucking, passenger, PERS, depot, and warehouse flows within the same shipment platform

### ❓ How Do I Register Typical Processes?

In Logistiqo, the main operational record is usually the **Shipment**. The business process is defined by the selected **Shipment Type**.

- **Where do I register a Goods Receipt?** Create a **Shipment** with a shipment type such as **Goods Receipt** or **Warehouse Receipt**, then register inbound goods against that shipment
- **Where do I register a Goods Issue?** Create a **Shipment** with the shipment type **Goods Issue**, then execute outbound allocation, picking, and goods issue from that shipment
- **Where do I register a normal trucking order?** Create a **Shipment** with the relevant road/trucking shipment type and maintain sender, receiver, goods, services, and rates there
- **Where do I register a passenger transport or PERS mission?** Create a **Shipment** with a passenger transport or **PERS** shipment type, then manage dispatch, operational details, and emergency-specific fields on that shipment
- **Where do I register planned warehouse goods?** In the related warehouse **Shipment**, using planned goods, allocated goods, and picking-list functionality linked to that shipment
- **Where do I register an AWB shipment?** Create a **Shipment** with an AWB-enabled shipment type, then use the AWB extension or AI import to fill the air-waybill-specific data
- **Where do I register a BOL shipment?** Create a **Shipment** with a BOL-enabled shipment type, then use the BOL extension or AI import to fill the bill-of-lading-specific data
- **Where do I create a manifest or tour?** Start from the relevant shipments and generate the transport manifest/tour through the planning and optimization flow

## Warehouse Shipment Fulfillment in Logistiqo

In Logistiqo, warehouse functionality is fully integrated into the **shipment process**. This means there is no separate warehouse module — instead, all warehouse activities are handled directly within shipments.

### How Fulfillment Works

1. **Create a Shipment**

   - Start by creating a shipment in **Shipments**
   - Define sender, receiver, and any intermediate stops

2. **Inbound (Goods Receipt)**

   - Goods are received and assigned to the shipment
   - Items can be scanned (barcode) or entered manually
   - The system registers the goods as available in the warehouse context

3. **Storage / Inventory**

   - Goods are logically stored as part of the shipment
   - You always know:

     - what items are available
     - which shipment they belong to
   - Optional: assign warehouse locations (bins, zones)

4. **Picking (Commissioning)**

   - When preparing outbound shipments, items are picked from available stock
   - The system ensures correct quantities and traceability

5. **Outbound (Shipping)**

   - Picked goods are assigned to the outbound shipment
   - Goods are marked as shipped and leave the warehouse

6. **Full Traceability**

   - Every step is linked to the shipment:

     - inbound → storage → picking → outbound
   - This ensures complete transparency and tracking

## Statement Processing & Mail Run

The statement process works in two steps:

### 1. Statement Batch Creation

First, you generate the statements.

- The system suggests a default date range for the batch.
- You can adjust the dates if needed.
- When you start the batch, PDF statements are created and stored.
- At the same time, a new record is created in the `Statement` table for each statement.

### 2. Statement Mail

After creation, you send the statements.

- The `Statement Mail` screen shows all generated statements.
- Each record indicates whether it has already been emailed or not.
- You can filter by date range.
- You can choose how the email is sent:
  - With PDF attachment
  - With a download link only

This allows you to first generate all statements in a controlled batch, and then send them separately and flexibly.
